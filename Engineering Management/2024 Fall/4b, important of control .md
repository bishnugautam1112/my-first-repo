### 1. Solution to the Asked Question (For Exam)

**Q. 4. b) Why is control important in an organization? Explain how it ensures the successful execution of engineering projects. (7 Marks)**

**Answer:**

**Introduction to Control:**
In management, "control" is the systematic process of monitoring actual performance, comparing it against pre-established standards or goals, identifying deviations, and taking corrective actions. It is the final function of management that ensures plans are actually being achieved.

**Part 1: Why is Control Important in an Organization?**
Control is a vital function for any organization for the following reasons:
1. **Ensures Alignment with Organizational Goals:** By continuously monitoring performance, managers can ensure that daily operations and employee outputs are moving in the direction of the company's strategic objectives.
2. **Improves Performance and Quality:** Regular control mechanisms (like quality control checks and performance appraisals) provide feedback. This helps employees improve their skills and ensures the final product meets required standards.
3. **Identifies Training Needs:** Analyzing performance data reveals gaps in employee skills. If a team is consistently failing to meet targets, control highlights the need for additional training or resources.
4. **Motivates Employees:** Fair and transparent control systems foster accountability. When employees know their performance is measured and good work is recognized, their motivation increases.

**Part 2: How Control Ensures the Successful Execution of Engineering Projects**
Engineering projects (like software development or infrastructure building) are highly complex, budget-sensitive, and time-bound. Control ensures their success in the following ways:
1. **Early Detection of Deviations (Risk Management):** Engineering projects often face technical glitches or schedule delays. Control systems track daily progress so that project managers can identify these deviations early and take corrective action before minor issues become critical failures.
2. **Effective Resource Allocation:** Using ICT control tools (like Jira, Wrike, or Microsoft Project), managers can monitor team capacity and budget expenditures in real-time. This ensures engineers are not overloaded and funds are utilized efficiently.
3. **Task and Schedule Management:** Control involves setting clear milestones (e.g., using Gantt charts). It ensures that dependent tasks are completed on time so the entire engineering project pipeline flows smoothly without bottlenecks.
4. **Quality Assurance (QA):** In engineering, a small defect can cause a system crash. Continuous control (through automated testing, CI/CD pipelines, and code reviews) ensures that the software or product is reliable, functional, and meets client requirements before final deployment.

**Conclusion:**
Without control, even the best plans can fail. In an organization, control acts as a steering wheel, and in engineering projects, it acts as a safety net that guarantees projects are delivered on time, within budget, and with the highest quality.
