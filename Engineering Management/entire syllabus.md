### 1.1 Management
**Exam Note (English):**
*   **Definition:** The process of organizing, planning, leading, and controlling resources to achieve organizational goals efficiently.
*   **1.1.1 Functions:** 
    *   **P**lanning (Setting goals)
    *   **O**rganizing (Arranging resources)

# üöÄ UNIT 2: PLANNING AND ORGANIZING (6 Hrs)

### 2.1 Planning
**Exam Note (English):**
*   **Definition:** Thinking about and organizing activities to achieve a goal. Bridging the gap from where we are to where we want to go.
*   **2.1.1 Levels of Planning:**
    *   **Strategic:** Long-term (3-5+ yrs), whole company, done by Top Level (e.g., "Become #1 AI App").
    *   **Tactical:** Medium-term (1-3 yrs), specific departments, done by Middle Level (e.g., "Hire 50 AI devs").
    *   **Operational:** Short-term (daily/weekly), specific tasks, done by Lower Level (e.g., "Code the login page today").
*   **2.1.2 Steps in Planning:** Set Objectives $\rightarrow$ Identify Options $\rightarrow$ Determine Resources $\rightarrow$ Create Timeline $\rightarrow$ Execute.
*   **2.1.3 Tools for Planning:**
    *   *SWOT Analysis:* Strengths, Weaknesses, Opportunities, Threats.
    *   *Gantt Charts:* Visual project timeline bars.
    *   *Software:* Jira (Agile/Software), Microsoft Project, Trello.
*   **2.1.4 Importance:** Provides direction, reduces uncertainty/risk, prevents waste of resources.

üí° **Neplish Concept:** Planning bhane ko map herne kaam ho. Mathi ko boss le 5 barsa ko lamo plan banaucha (Strategic), department le 1 barsa ko (Tactical), ra team le aaja-bholi ko (Operational). Tools ma Jira ra SWOT yaad rakhne.
üß† **Memory Technique:** 
*   Levels: **STO** (Stop) $\rightarrow$ **S**trategic, **T**actical, **O**perational.
*   SWOT = Internal (S, W) + External (O, T).

---

### 2.2 Organizing
**Exam Note (English):**
*   **Definition:** Assigning tasks, allocating resources, and establishing a hierarchy to achieve the plan.
*   **2.2.1 Process:** Divide Work $\rightarrow$ Departmentalization (Group tasks) $\rightarrow$ Assign Duties $\rightarrow$ Delegate Authority.
*   **2.2.3 Types of Organizational Structure (üö® VERY IMPORTANT):**
    *   *Traditional:*
        *   **Line:** Military style. Direct vertical command. Simple but slow for large companies.
        *   **Functional:** Grouped by skills (e.g., HR, IT, Marketing). Deep expertise but poor cross-team communication (silos).
    *   *Modern:*
        *   **Matrix:** Dual reporting (2 bosses). Employee reports to Functional Manager + Project Manager. Highly flexible for complex tech projects.
        *   **Network:** Core functions kept in-house; everything else is outsourced (e.g., Outsource server hosting to AWS).
        *   **Hybrid:** Combines structures (e.g., Functional for HR, but Team-based for R&D).
        *   **Team-Based/Flat:** No middle managers. Self-managing agile teams (e.g., Scrum teams).

üí° **Neplish Concept:** Kaam kasari baadne? **Matrix** ma 2 ta boss huncha (department ko boss ra project ko boss). **Network** ma aafu main kaam garne, aru sab thekka (outsource) dine. **Flat** ma boss nai nahune, sab equal.
üß† **Memory Technique:** 
*   Matrix = 2 Bosses (Like the movie, bending rules). 
*   Network = Web of Outsourcing.

    *   *Rapid Tech Obsolescence:* 5-year plans fail because technology changes every 6 months.

# üöÄ UNIT 3: MOTIVATION AND LEADERSHIP (6 Hrs)

### 3.1 Motivation & Its Theories
**Exam Note (English):**
*   **Definition:** The internal process or force that energizes, directs, and sustains behavior toward achieving a goal. Derived from the Latin word *"movere"* (to move).
*   **3.1.1 Theories of Motivation:**
    *   **1. Maslow‚Äôs Hierarchy of Needs:** A 5-tier pyramid. Lower-level needs must be fulfilled before moving up. 
        *   *Levels:* Physiological (food/salary) $\rightarrow$ Safety (job security) $\rightarrow$ Social (teamwork/friends) $\rightarrow$ Esteem (recognition) $\rightarrow$ Self-Actualization (reaching full potential/creative work).
    *   **2. Herzberg‚Äôs Two-Factor Theory:** Motivation depends on two distinct factors:
        *   *Hygiene Factors:* Salary, company policies, physical working conditions. (If absent = dissatisfaction. If present = neutral, *does not* motivate).
        *   *Motivator Factors:* Achievement, recognition, responsibility, growth. (These actually drive people to work harder).
    *   **3. Vroom's Expectancy Theory:** Motivation is a math formula based on belief.
        *   *Formula:* **Motivation = Expectancy (E) √ó Instrumentality (I) √ó Valence (V)**
        *   *(E)* "Can I do it?" $\times$ *(I)* "Will I get a reward?" $\times$ *(V)* "Do I value the reward?"
    *   **4. Adam's Equity Theory:** Employees are motivated when they perceive a balance (fairness) between their *Inputs* (effort, time) and *Outputs* (salary, respect) compared to their coworkers.
*   **3.1.2 Techniques for Motivation:**
    *   Provide financial incentives (bonuses).
    *   Offer professional development (training).
    *   Encourage employee autonomy and empowerment.
    *   Provide regular constructive feedback and praise.

üí° **Neplish Concept:** Motivation bhaneko kaam garna man lagne energy ho. Exam ma 4 ta theory pakka sodhcha. **Maslow** le bharyang banayo (paila paisa, ani ijjat). **Herzberg** le bhanyo salary le matra khusi didaina, promotion ra ijjat le didcha. **Expectancy** le formula diyo (E x I x V). **Equity** le sathi sanga aafno salary compare garera fair chha ki chaina hercha.
üß† **Memory Technique:** 
*   Theories: **MHEE** $\rightarrow$ **M**aslow (Pyramid), **H**erzberg (2 Factors), **E**xpectancy (Formula), **E**quity (Fairness/Balance).
*   Maslow Levels: **PSS-ES** (Paisa, Safety, Social, Esteem, Self-actualization).

### 3.2 Leadership
**Exam Note (English):**
*   **Definition:** The act of influencing, guiding, and empowering a group of people to achieve a common organizational goal.
*   **3.2.1 Leadership Styles:**
    *   **Autocratic:** Leader makes decisions independently without team input. Rule-oriented. (Pros: Fast decisions in crises. Cons: Kills creativity and morale).
    *   **Democratic:** Leader makes decisions collaboratively. Includes team in discussions. (Pros: High trust and morale. Cons: Very time-consuming).
    *   **Servant:** Leader puts the team's needs first. Focuses on mentoring, empowering, and supporting employee well-being. (Pros: High retention. Cons: May struggle with rapid execution).
    *   **Transformational:** Leader inspires and motivates the team to innovate and disrupt. High energy and visionary. (Pros: Drives massive growth. Cons: Can lead to team burnout).
*   **3.2.2 Learning Organization in ICT:**
    *   An organization that continuously learns, adapts, and transforms itself to survive rapid tech changes (Concept by Peter Senge).
    *   *Characteristics:* Continuous learning, open communication, knowledge sharing, and empowering employees to experiment without fear of failure.

üí° **Neplish Concept:** Leader bhane ko boss matra haina, bato dekhaune manche ho. **Autocratic** = Strict (Sunne matra). **Democratic** = Salla garne. **Servant** = Team ko sewa garne/Support garne. **Transformational** = Josh dine ra naya kura (innovation) garne. Tech company haru **Learning Org** hunu parcha, jasle naya kura sikcha ra change huna darau-daina.
üß† **Memory Technique:** 
*   Leadership Styles: **D-A-S-T** $\rightarrow$ **D**emocratic, **A**utocratic, **S**ervant, **T**ransformational.

# üöÄ UNIT 4: HRM AND CONTROL (8 Hrs)

### 4.1 Human Resource Management (HRM)
**Exam Note (English):**
*   **Definition:** The systematic approach to recruiting, hiring, developing, and managing employees to achieve organizational goals. It bridges the gap between the organization's needs and its workforce.
*   **4.1.1 Functions of HRM:**

    3.  **Performance Appraisal:** Evaluating job performance and giving feedback.
    4.  **Compensation:** Managing salaries, bonuses, and benefits.

üí° **Neplish Concept:** HRM bhaneko manche (Human Resource) ko management ho. Yesko main kaam: Khali thau ma manche **Lyaune** (Recruit), uniharu lai **Sikaune** (Train), uniharu ko kaam **Herne** (Appraise), ra **Paisa/Khusi** dine (Compensation).

### 4.1.2 Job Analysis: Description vs. Specification (üö® Important)
**Exam Note (English):**
*   **Job Analysis:** The process of gathering detailed information about a specific job. It produces two outputs:
    1.  **Job Description (JD):** Focuses on the **JOB**. Lists duties, responsibilities, working conditions, and tasks. (e.g., "Write code in Java, debug errors").
    2.  **Job Specification (JS):** Focuses on the **PERSON**. Lists required qualifications, skills, education, and experience. (e.g., "Bachelor's in IT, 2 years experience").

üí° **Neplish Concept:**
*   **Description (JD):** K kaam garne? (Duties).
*   **Specification (JS):** K qualification chahincha? (Skills/Degree).
üß† **Memory Technique:** 
*   **JD** = **D**uties.
*   **JS** = **S**kills.

### 4.1.4 & 4.1.5 Training & Appraisal
**Exam Note (English):**
*   **Training Types:**
    *   **On-the-Job:** Learning while working (e.g., Mentoring, Job Rotation). Practical and cheap.
    *   **Off-the-Job:** Learning away from work (e.g., Workshops, Online Courses, Simulations). Theoretical and focused.
*   **Performance Appraisal Methods:**
    *   **MBO (Management by Objectives):** Boss and employee agree on goals together; evaluated on results.
    *   **Rating Scales:** Grading employees on criteria like punctuality, teamwork (1-5 scale).
    *   **360-Degree Feedback:** Feedback from boss, peers, subordinates, and clients.

üí° **Neplish Concept:**

### 4.2 Control
**Exam Note (English):**
*   **Definition:** The process of monitoring performance, comparing it with goals, and correcting deviations to ensure objectives are met.
*   **4.2.1 Importance:**
    *   Ensures project stays on track (Time/Cost).
    *   Detects errors early (Quality Control).
    *   Helps in resource allocation and avoiding waste.
*   **4.2.2 The Control Process (4 Steps):**
    1.  **Set Standards:** Define the goal (e.g., "Finish code in 2 days").
    2.  **Measure Performance:** Check actual work (e.g., "Only 50% finished").
    3.  **Compare:** Compare Actual vs. Standard (e.g., "We are behind schedule").
    4.  **Corrective Action:** Fix the problem (e.g., "Add more devs or work overtime").

üí° **Neplish Concept:** Control bhaneko gadi ko break ra steering jastai ho. Plan ta banayo, tara tyo anusar kaam bhayo ki nai? Check garne process ho.
üß† **Memory Technique:** Process = **S-M-C-C** $\rightarrow$ **S**et, **M**easure, **C**ompare, **C**orrect.

# üöÄ UNIT 5: EMERGING TRENDS IN ENGINEERING MANAGEMENT (4 Hrs)

### 5.1 Modern Management Concepts
**Exam Note (English):**
*   **Participative Management:** A leadership style where employees at all levels are encouraged to contribute ideas and participate in the decision-making process. (Result: Higher commitment and better decisions).
*   **Conflict Resolution:** The process of resolving disputes within a team.
    *   *Techniques:* **Collaboration** (Win-Win, working together), **Compromise** (Give and Take), **Accommodating** (Letting the other person win), **Avoiding** (Ignoring the issue).
*   **Change Management:** A structured approach to preparing and supporting individuals and teams to adopt organizational changes (e.g., switching to new software). It minimizes resistance.
*   **Innovation vs. Disruption:**
    *   **Innovation:** Creating new methods, ideas, or products (Improvement).
    *   **Disruption:** A specific type of innovation that creates a new market and eventually displaces established market leaders (e.g., Netflix disrupting DVD rentals).

üí° **Neplish Concept:**
*   **Participative:** Staff lai "Timro bichar k cha?" bhanera sodhne.
*   **Conflict:** Jhagada huda **Collaboration** (Milera solution nikalne) sab bhanda ramro ho.
*   **Disruption:** Purano market lai d ‡§ß‡•ç‡§µ‡§∏‡•ç‡§§ banaune naya idea (Jastai: Pathao le Taxi lai garyo).
üß† **Memory Technique:** Conflict Styles = **CAC-C** $\rightarrow$ **C**ollaborate, **A**void, **C**ompromise, **C**ompete.

### 5.2 Recent Engineering Trends (Agile & DevOps)
**Exam Note (English):**
*   **Agile Methodologies:** An iterative approach to project management (specifically software) where projects are broken into small pieces called "Sprints". It focuses on continuous delivery and flexibility rather than rigid long-term planning.
*   **DevOps (Development + Operations):** A set of practices that combines Software Development (Dev) and IT Operations (Ops). It aims to shorten the development lifecycle and provide continuous delivery with high software quality.
*   **Data-Driven Decision Making:** Using actual data analytics and metrics to guide strategic business decisions rather than relying on intuition or observation.

üí° **Neplish Concept:**
*   **Agile:** Ekai choti thulo software nabaunne. Choto-choto tukra (Sprint) ma banaune, check garne, ani feri banaune.
*   **DevOps:** Code lekhne (Dev) ra Server herne (Ops) sathi haru milera kaam garne.
*   **Data-Driven:** Hawa taal ma decision naline, Data herera garne.
