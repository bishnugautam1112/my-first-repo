### 1. Change Management & Lewin’s Model (Highly Expected - 7/8 Marks)
**Q: Define change management and explain its significance in an ICT-driven organization. Discuss the steps involved in the change management process.**

**1. Definition:** 
Change management is the structured approach to transitioning individuals, teams, and organizations from a current state to a desired future state to fulfill a vision or strategy.

**2. Significance in ICT Organizations:**
*   **Technological Obsolescence:** Tech stacks (languages, frameworks) change rapidly. Companies must adapt or die.
*   **Agility:** Helps software companies pivot quickly in response to competitor features (e.g., Instagram adding Reels to combat TikTok).
*   **Reduces Resistance:** Engineers often resist changes to their preferred coding environments or processes. Change management smooths this friction.

**3. The Process (Lewin’s 3-Step Model) - *Draw a simple 3-box arrow diagram for this!***
*   **Step 1: Unfreeze (Preparation):** Breaking down the existing status quo. Creating a sense of urgency and explaining *why* the change is necessary. Overcoming initial resistance.
*   **Step 2: Change (Implementation):** The actual transition. This involves heavy communication, training, and support as employees learn the new system.
*   **Step 3: Refreeze (Solidification):** Reinforcing the change so it becomes the new standard. Updating company policies and rewarding employees who adapt.

> 🏆 **A+ ICT Example:** "When a Nepalese telecom company migrates its legacy database to **AWS Cloud**, the manager first *unfreezes* by showing how the old servers are failing. During the *change*, developers are trained on AWS. Finally, the company *refreezes* by making AWS certification a requirement for future promotions."

### 2. Innovation Management vs. Disruption (8 Marks)
**Q: Explain innovation management and disruption. How can an engineering manager balance innovation and operational stability?**

*Always use a table when comparing two concepts.*

| Feature | Innovation Management | Digital Disruption |
| :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Definition** | The process of incrementally improving existing products, processes, or services. | A radical shift that creates a completely new market, rendering the old industry obsolete. |
| **Pace** | Gradual and continuous. | Sudden and aggressive. |
| **Risk Level** | Low to Moderate. | Extremely High. |
| **ICT Example** | Upgrading a telecom network from 4G to 5G. | **WhatsApp** disrupting the SMS industry, or **eSewa** disrupting physical bank branches. |

**How to Balance Stability and Innovation (The 70-20-10 Rule):**
To maintain competitiveness, an engineering manager should allocate resources using the **70-20-10 framework**:
*   **70% Core Business (Stability):** Focus on maintaining and slightly improving the current cash-cow products (e.g., fixing bugs in the main app).
*   **20% Adjacent Innovation:** Expanding existing business into new related markets.
*   **10% Disruptive/Transformational:** High-risk R&D for completely new, game-changing ideas (e.g., Google’s "20% time" policy that led to Gmail).

### 3. Conflict Resolution Mechanics (7/8 Marks)
**Q: Describe common techniques for conflict resolution. Differentiate between Negotiation, Mediation, and Arbitration.**

**1. What is Conflict?**
A clash of interests or ideas. In engineering, *Task Conflict* (debating the best software architecture) is healthy, but *Relationship Conflict* (personal attacks) destroys productivity.

**2. Core Resolution Techniques (The Differences):**

| Feature | Negotiation | Mediation | Arbitration |
| :--- | :--- | :--- | :--- |
| **Third Party Involvement?** | NO. Only the disputing parties are involved. | YES. A neutral 3rd party is present. | YES. A neutral 3rd party is present. |
| **Power of 3rd Party** | N/A | **Facilitator.** The mediator only helps them talk. Cannot force a decision. | **Judge.** The arbitrator makes a binding, final decision. |
| **Formality** | Highly informal. | Semi-formal. | Highly formal (like a mini-court). |
| **ICT Scenario** | Frontend and Backend devs discussing API endpoints. | Scrum Master helping two devs resolve a dispute over sprint workload. | Company management deciding which team gets the server budget. |

> 🏆 **A+ Closer:** "In Agile engineering teams, managers should first encourage **Negotiation** to foster self-organizing teams, using **Mediation** only when the team is deadlocked, and saving **Arbitration** as an absolute last resort, as it creates a 'win-lose' dynamic."

### 4. Participative Management (Short Note 5 Marks / Full Q 7 Marks)
**Q: Discuss the concept of participative management and its impact on employee motivation in ICT organizations.**

**1. Concept:**
Participative management is a leadership philosophy where employees are actively involved in the organization's decision-making processes, problem-solving, and goal-setting, rather than just taking orders from the top.

**2. Key Characteristics:**
*   Decentralization of power.
*   Two-way, transparent communication.
*   Open-door policy and collaborative culture.

**3. Impact on Motivation in ICT (The "Why it works" part):**
*   **Fulfills Higher-Level Needs:** According to Maslow, participation fulfills *Esteem* and *Self-Actualization* needs. Software engineers are knowledge workers; they want to be heard, not commanded.
*   **Psychological Ownership:** If a developer helps design the system architecture, they are intrinsically motivated to write better code because it is "their" idea.
*   **Agile Synergy:** The entire **Agile/Scrum framework** is built on participative management. Ceremonies like *Sprint Planning* (team decides what they can achieve) and *Retrospectives* (team decides how to improve) are pure participative management in action.
